Getting Around
Japan transport essentials for the family

🚃 IC Card (Suica / Pasmo)

The essential tap-and-go card for all trains, buses and metro. Works across Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka and most of Japan.

Pick up at any JR station arrivals hall or add to Apple Wallet before you fly.

💡 Load ¥3,000–5,000 per person on arrival. Top up at any ticket machine.

🚄 Shinkansen (Bullet Train)

Tokyo → Kyoto takes ~2h 15min on the Nozomi. Book reserved seats in advance — especially in cherry blossom season.

💡 Sit on the right side (D/E seats) Tokyo → Kyoto for Mt Fuji views on a clear day.

🚕 Taxis

Plentiful, metered and honest. No tipping required. Doors open and close automatically — don't touch them.

💡 Use GO app (Japan's Uber equivalent) to book ahead and pay by card.

🚇 Tokyo Metro

Covers almost everywhere in central Tokyo. Google Maps works perfectly for routing.

💡 Stand to the left on escalators in Tokyo, right in Osaka.

🚌 Hakone Freepass

Covers Romancecar train from Shinjuku + all transport within Hakone. Buy at Odakyu counter at Shinjuku or digitally on your phone.
